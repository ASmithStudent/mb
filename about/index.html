About the course

This course, Introduction to Web Development, is brought to you by Mission Bit, a nonproft organization based in San Francisco. According to their mission statement:

Mission Bit strives to eliminate the tech divide for youth living in urban poverty and rural areas across the San Francisco Bay Area by building computer programming and professional opportunity pathways for public school students.

To this end, this course strives to engage students by offering a variety of fun and creative projects, exploring web design through peer to peer collaboration. The skills we'll use are essential for nearly all engineers, developers, and people working with computers, and impact all of our lives on a daily basis. Students will work with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript and can use these languages as a stepping stone to following their own pathways in computer science.

Beyond computer science, students will develop many other life skills, such as team work and peer skills, overcoming obstacles and solving problems, developing a positive growth mindset, and using planning and focus to accomplish goals.

About the Instructor

Greg Gorlen holds a BA in Political Science from San Francisco State University and a variety of certificates in Computer Science from City College of San Francisco. By day, he's a library assistant at the San Francisco Public Library. His programming journey began in 2015, when he enrolled at CCSF and began exploring games, puzzles, Twitter bots, web scraping, and a variety of other adventures in code. He also works as a teacher's assistant and tutor while leading Mission Bit classes.
